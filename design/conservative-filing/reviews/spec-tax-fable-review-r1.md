# Conservative-filing SPEC — tax-correctness lens review r1 — NOT GREEN (2 Critical + 2 Important)

_Fable, independent, tax-correctness lens. Verified vs the 2025 IRS Form 8949 instructions, Notices
2025-7/2026-20, §§1001/1011/1222/1223, §6662(d), and the engine source. Also surfaces a SHIPPED-APP bug
(8949 boxes for TY2025 digital assets) as spillover._

---

VERDICT: NOT GREEN

## Findings

**1. [Critical] D-6 / P1 / D-4+P7 — every tranche disposal is unconditionally mapped to Part II (long-term), with no guard that the window-end is more than one year before the disposal.** Character is determined by the holding period (§1222(1)/(3): long-term requires held *more than 1 year*; §1223 day-after convention). D-2 correctly sets `acquired_at` = window END (conservative — never overclaims the hold), but nothing in P1's input bounds the window, so a tranche with a window ending within 1 year of a sale is SHORT-term — and D-6 says "reports on Part II (long-term)" unconditionally, P1's tests pin "long-term on disposal," and D-4/P7's canned disclosure hard-codes "reported long-term." Filing a short-term gain on Part II claims preferential LT rates → **understated tax** — the one direction this feature must never err, and exactly what its "nothing to adjust upward" guarantee forbids. Note the SPEC here contradicts its own engine: `fold.rs:72` (`term_for` → `is_long_term(start, disposed)`) and `forms.rs` (Part I/II from `leg.term`, Box **C** for ST) already derive term correctly per-leg. The i8949 "VARIOUS" rule itself commands the split: "you must still report the short-term gain (or loss)… on Part I and the long-term… on Part II." Concrete scenario: window `[2024-06-01, 2025-03-01]`, sale 2025-09-01 → 6-month hold → ST (up to 37%) filed as LT (≤20%). Fix: derive Part/box from the computed term (ST → Part I with the ST box analogues) and make D-6/P1/P7 conditional — or hard-refuse/warn any tranche whose window-end is not >1 year before its first disposal.

**2. [Critical] D-6's box mapping (E/F) is wrong for the very tax years this feature files (2025+), and its code-grounding claim is false.** The 2025 Form 8949 added digital-asset-specific boxes — Part I: **G** (1099-DA, basis reported), **H** (1099-DA, basis not reported), **I** (no 1099-DA); Part II: **J/K/L** analogues — and the i8949 (2025) says expressly: *"Use box J, K, or L to report long-term digital asset transactions. Do not use box F to report long-term digital asset transactions"* (and G/H/I, not C, for short-term). So the correct tranche mapping for TY2025+ is: no 1099-DA (private/P2P/self-custody) → **Box L**, broker 1099-DA without basis → **Box K** (ST: I / H) — not F/E. D-6's "verified against IRS i8949" was evidently verified against a pre-2025 revision. Second, the reuse claim is false: `forms.rs`'s `Form8949Box` enum contains **only C and F**, and its doc comment says the opposite of what D-6 cites it for — *"We NEVER auto-assign A/B (ST) or D/E (LT): asserting a 1099-B was issued / basis reported would fabricate an unsubstantiated box"* — `box_needs_review` is a review flag, not an E-vs-F driver, so "P1 reuses it, adds no new box logic" cannot produce even the SPEC's own (wrong) Box E. The fix needs real design: year-aware box selection (C/F remain correct for pre-2025 tax years; G–L from TY2025), and D-6 should keep the existing review-flag semantics rather than auto-asserting a 1099-DA from custody alone (a foreign/non-filing exchange disposal marked K would fabricate a received-1099 claim — the code comment's own rationale). **Spillover beyond this SPEC: the shipped app emits C/F for ALL digital-asset rows, which is already contrary to the 2025 instructions for TY2025 filings — file as its own follow-up.**

**3. [Important] D-2's floor ("lowest daily close in the window") is not a floor, and "the most conservative non-zero basis" is a mis-statement.** A daily-close minimum can sit well above the window's intraday low (e.g., March 2020: lowest BTC daily close ≈ $4.9k vs intraday low ≈ $3.8k — a ~22% gap). A filer who actually bought an intraday dip below every close in the window has actual basis **below** the "floor," so the floor overstates basis → understates gain → the indefensible direction, and the "at least the window low" defensibility argument collapses on exam. In v1 nothing >$0 is filed (D-7), so no wrong v1 filing — but D-2 is the resolved definition Approach B will file, P5 encodes it, and P6's "save ~$X" overstates the defensibly-recoverable delta. Fix: define the floor over daily **lows** (if the bundled dataset has OHLC), or apply a haircut / rename it ("close-based reference price") and state the caveat in D-2 and the P6 copy.

**4. [Important] D-4 overstates penalty protection for the floor position: "A conservative $0/floor basis is the fallback… so no §6662(d)/Form 8275 disclosure is required."** True for **$0** — a $0 basis cannot understate gain (§1001/§1011), so no basis-attributable underpayment and nothing for §6662 to attach to. **False for a filed floor**: an estimated >$0 basis, if disallowed on exam (substantiation is the taxpayer's burden), produces an underpayment; the §6662(d) substantial-understatement penalty is then avoided only via substantial authority OR reasonable basis **plus adequate disclosure** (§6662(d)(2)(B)(ii)) — which means Form 8275 (or Rev.-Proc.-specified on-return disclosure), *not* a free-form attached memo. An estimated window-low likely clears "reasonable basis" but not clearly "substantial authority," so the 8275 is precisely the protection a floor-filer needs. Scope D-4's no-8275 conclusion to the $0 position and note that B's promote-to-filed-floor path should generate/recommend a Form 8275. (P7's free-form statement is good audit hygiene and satisfies the i8949 attachment ask, but it has no §6662 legal effect.)

**5. [Minor] D-1 vs D-2 internal ambiguity with a tax consequence:** D-1 sets the tranche `utc_timestamp` = "era-representative date"; D-2 sets the holding-period date = window **end**. The engine's term flows from the Acquire timestamp → `lot.acquired_at` → `term_for`. Pin `utc_timestamp := window end` explicitly — an implementer choosing a midpoint "representative" date would overclaim the holding period.

**6. [Minor] D-1/P1's "the 8949 'how acquired' column" — Form 8949 has no such column.** How-acquired is Form **8283** (donations; `forms.rs::how_acquired_from`). The real 8949 effect is col (b) = VARIOUS. The SPEC is also silent on the donation surface: `Form8283HowAcquired` needs an `EstimatedConservative` arm (compile-forced; presumably `Review`), and the §170(e) interplay should be stated/tested — LT tranche donation → FMV deduction (a $0 basis doesn't limit it); ST-held tranche donation → deduction limited to basis = **$0**.

**7. [Minor] The no-loss invariant is true but unstated:** a $0-basis tranche disposal can never produce a loss (gain = proceeds − $0 ≥ 0), so no §1211/§1212 loss-limit or §1091 interaction can ever arise from a v1 tranche — worth an explicit KAT. For the future floor path, state that a loss must never be claimed off an *estimated* basis (disallowance flips a claimed loss into a gain — maximally assailable). Verified: §1091 still does not apply to directly-held digital assets as of 2026 (property, not "stock or securities"; no enacted extension), consistent with `forms.rs`'s col (f) comment.

**8. [Minor] Gift/inherited coins lumped into a $0 tranche never produce an indefensible result** ($0 ≤ §1015 carryover and ≤ §1014 FMV-at-death; window-end only *shortens* the hold vs §1223(2) tacking; inherited property is LT **by law**, §1223(9), so an ST mis-classification only overpays) — but three refinements: (a) inherited basis is reconstructable by law from date-of-death FMV with **no purchase records**, the cheapest reconstruction for this persona — P6's nudge should say so; (b) i8949 directs "INHERITED" in col (b) for known-inherited property; (c) P7's statement must stay provenance-neutral (not assert "purchases in the window") for coins the filer knows were gifted/inherited.

**9. [Nit] D-6's i8949 quote is real but clipped:** the sentence "If you don't use the actual cost, attach an explanation of your basis" follows an enumeration (inherited / gift / tax-free exchange / wash sale). Treating it as mandating P7 for the $0-unsubstantiated case is the correct conservative reading — keep P7 required — but cite the context so the "mandates" claim is precise.

**10. [Nit] Rev. Proc. 2024-28 / §1.1012-1(j):** specify that a tranche is declared into a specific wallet (per-wallet tracking binds 2025+ dispositions). With $0 basis the safe-harbor allocation is moot; note for B that constructing a >$0 basis into a wallet in 2026 sits outside the Rev. Proc. 2024-28 allocation window.

## Verified sound (no finding)
- **G-1 / private sales:** correct — omission is the evasion line; a P2P disposal is fully reportable, amount realized = money + FMV of property/services received (§1001(b)); box is **L** for 2025+ (per finding 2); fee-netting matches the 1099-DA gross-proceeds definition (§1.6045-1).
- **G-2 / $0 default:** correct — basis substantiation is the taxpayer's burden; unproven basis = $0 is the IRS's own fallback; a $0 basis can never understate gain, so no basis-driven §6662 exposure. "Unassailable" holds for the basis *component*; character and proceeds remain assailable — which is why finding 1 gates.
- **D-2 window-end holding period:** conservative and defensible; the engine's day-after §1223 convention applies.
- **D-3/P4:** consistent with §1.1012-1(j) and Notices 2025-7/2026-20 — relief runs through **2026-12-31**; 2027+ broker-held units need broker-communicated ID or default to FIFO; own-books works for self-custody every year; wallet-at-disposal is the right key; `optimize.rs::persistability` already encodes it.
- **Transition facts (DESIGN §5):** all correct (per-wallet 2025; 1099-DA proceeds 2025 / basis 2026; own-transfers non-taxable w/ carryover; §1014 step-up).
- **D-7:** filing $0 (pure overpayment) is always permissible — no rule compels claiming a known-favorable basis; basis is not a §446 method.
- **VARIOUS in col (b):** explicitly extended to digital assets in the 2025 i8949 — correct, subject to the ST/LT split (finding 1).
- **No adjustment code:** correct — code B corrects a basis *shown* on a 1099-B/DA; supplying a missing basis in col (e) under box H/K (or I/L) takes no code and a zero col (g).
- **D-5 clean export:** consistent; no tax defect. State tax out of scope acceptable (note in product copy).

Bottom line: the conservative architecture ($0 default, window-end holding, steer-documented-first, mandatory methodology disclosure, never-omit) is tax-sound. What blocks: the unguarded always-long-term mapping (1), the stale pre-2025 8949 box scheme plus a false code-grounding claim (2), a floor that isn't a floor (3), and an overbroad no-8275 claim for the future floor position (4).

Sources: IRS i8949 (2025); Notice 2026-20; §§1001/1011/1222/1223/1091/6662(d).
