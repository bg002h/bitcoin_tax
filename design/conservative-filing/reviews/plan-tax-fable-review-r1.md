## Findings

**I-1. [Important] Task 15 / SPEC §6 invariant — the no-loss KAT is NOT airtight, and the planned fixtures cannot falsify it. Two reachable engine paths put a loss (or a >$0 basis) on a filed $0-tranche 8949 row.**

The task asserts "gain = proceeds − $0 ≥ 0" with fixtures at "any positive proceeds" and "$0 proceeds" — both fee-free. Against the actual engine:

- **(a) USD-fee netting.** `fold.rs:634`: `net = round_cents(*proceeds - *fee_usd)`. A disposal whose `fee_usd > usd_proceeds` (a dust sale with a minimum exchange fee; a `ReclassifyOutflow{Dispose}` P2P sale with `fee_usd`) produces **negative net proceeds**, allocated onto the legs by `make_disposal_legs` (fold.rs:134-140, last-leg remainder) → a $0-basis tranche leg with `gain < 0` — a filed **loss**, which then *does* enter §1211/§1212 netting, contradicting the KAT's claim verbatim.
- **(b) TP8(c) fee-sat basis re-home.** `consume_fee` (fold.rs:323-374) draws on-chain fee sats FIFO and, under the user-mandated treatment (c), re-homes their **documented** basis onto the **last** disposal leg (`rehome_onto_disposal_leg`, fold.rs:313-316: `leg.basis += carry; leg.gain = proceeds − basis`). Under HIFO the $0 tranche fragment is consumed **last** (`hifo_cmp`, pools.rs:276-279), so the last leg is precisely the tranche leg: the filed 8949 tranche row shows **cost basis > $0** and its gain can go **negative** whenever the fee sats came from a documented lot (e.g., tranche exhausted by the principal, fee drawn from the remaining documented lot).

Neither filing **understates** tax — in (a) reported gain = P−F ≥ P−F−B_true, and in (b) the added basis is real, documented fee-sat basis, not the estimate — so this is not a G-4/D-7 breach in substance. The defect is plan-level: Task 15 would ship a green "invariant" KAT pinning a claim that is false on reachable inputs (the task's own step 2 says a negative gain "is a Critical — STOP", yet its fixtures are chosen so the STOP can never fire), and P3's dip-advisory copy ("names… its $0 basis, the resulting gain") plus P7's disclosure ("$0 position") would misdescribe exactly these rows. Authority: §1001(b)/§1011 (fees reduce amount realized / basis is real), §1211/§1212; SPEC §6 invariant KAT; tax-r1 min-7.

**Fix (plan + one-line SPEC amendment):** (i) add the two fee-corner KATs (fee_usd > proceeds; TP8(c) carry onto a tranche leg) as characterization tests; (ii) scope the invariant to what is actually true and worth guaranteeing — *"a tranche leg can never file a loss attributable to the estimate: any negative gain is attributable solely to documented `fee_usd` / fee-sat basis; absent fees, gain = proceeds − $0 ≥ 0"*; (iii) make Task 9/13 copy state the row's basis **as filed** (the fee-carry case) rather than unconditionally "$0"; (iv) amend SPEC §6's unconditional wording (it currently states the invariant without the fee qualifier — a SPEC-owned sentence, so route it through the SPEC, not silently in code).

**I-2. [Important] Task 6 — the tranche-side record-time refusal is over-broad vs the green SPEC's P1 scope, and permanently locks a real persona out of the feature's mandatory-disclosure path.**

The plan refuses "recording a `DeclareTranche` when ANY in-force allocation exists." SPEC P1's litigated test is narrower: *"refuses a **pre-2025** declaration under ANY in-force Path-B allocation."* The hazard the exclusion exists for (Path B's `transition.rs:88` discard; the r3-New-1 inert-flip) touches **only** lots in the pre-2025 Universal residue: a tranche with `window_end ≥ 2025-01-01` folds directly into a per-wallet pool (`pool_key`, pools.rs:15-21) and no seed/discard path can ever reach it — zero Rev. Proc. 2024-28 interaction. As planned, a filer holding an **effective** allocation (irrevocable in-app — `resolve.rs:1280-1290`; irrevocable in law, Rev. Proc. 2024-28 §5.02) who also has undocumented **2025+** coins can never declare them: the refusal is permanent, the Task-5 backstop doesn't apply (pre-2025 residue only), and the realistic workaround — a hand-entered $0 `Acquire` — files the identical $0 rows **without** the `EstimatedConservative` tag, silently losing P7's MANDATORY methodology disclosure (D-4 / i8949 basis-explanation — the SPEC's own "hard gap"), P3, and P6. The refusal direction errs conservative on tax, but the task as written contradicts the green SPEC's scope and forecloses D-4's guarantee for exactly the mixed-records filer this feature serves.

**Fix:** scope the tranche-side refusal to `window_end < TRANSITION_DATE` (mirroring the backstop and SPEC P1's test); keep the allocation-side refusal keyed on pre-2025 tranches (correctly scoped in the plan already); add a KAT that a ≥2025-window tranche records cleanly alongside an effective allocation.
