## Q1 — Is the finding right? Yes. Here is the adversarial half first.

**The steelman for "mixed rounding is acceptable":** in practice the Service will not bounce a paper return because an attachment carries cents behind a whole-dollar schedule; its transcription tolerances absorb sub-dollar noise, and the rounding election's enforcement is soft. If our standard were "won't draw a notice," you could ship the mixed packet. I want that stated honestly so nobody later claims the IRS *mandates* this fix.

But that is not the governing standard, three times over:

1. **The election's own text is all-or-nothing.** 2024 i1040 p. 23 (cited at SPEC §3.1, `design/SPEC_full_return.md:119-120`, and in my prior guidance): round one amount, round them all, *on the return and its schedules*. Form 8949 and Schedule D each print the invitation on their face ("This form may be easier to complete if you round off cents to whole dollars" — TY2024 `schedule_d.pdf` Part I/II headers). There is no per-form carve-out, and MeF (e-file) requires whole dollars on essentially all amount fields — corroborating that the IRS's own machinery treats the election as return-wide.
2. **The forms cite each other, and cited figures must agree.** Schedule D line 3 is *defined* as "Totals for all transactions reported on Form(s) 8949 with Box C checked" (TY2024 `schedule_d.pdf`; Box C/F on the 2024 revision — the Box I/L digital-asset boxes are the 2025 form, `fill8949.rs:4-6`, and v1 is TY2024). Schedule 2 line 4 is "Self-employment tax. **Attach Schedule SE**" (`f1040s2.pdf` line 43). Schedule 1 line 15 is "Deductible part of self-employment tax. **Attach Schedule SE**" (`f1040s1.pdf`). Form 8959 line 8 is "Self-employment income from **Schedule SE (Form 1040), Part I, line 6**" (`f8959.pdf:27`). A packet in which Schedule D says 301,235 and the 8949 behind it totals 301,234.56 — or Schedule 2 L4 says 29,871 while the attached SE line 12 reads 29,870.85 — is a form disagreeing with the attachment it names as its source. That is exactly the defect class already gated twice (P5-C1; §63(f) boxes), and the codebase's own bar is a **$1** disagreement (`schedule_2_line11_takes_the_printed_8959_line_18_not_the_rounded_total`, `printed.rs:917-947`).
3. **The phase's restated exit condition** — "every figure on it is internally and mutually consistent (cross-foots, **ties to its attachments**)" (`CONTINUITY_P6.md:124-126`, my own language) — is unsatisfiable by a mixed packet.

**And the omission is independently gating even before rounding enters:** `PrintedReturn` (`packet.rs:294-311`) has no member for 8949, SE, or 8283, while SPEC §7.1 names all three in the fill set (`design/SPEC_full_return.md:416-417`). The anti-drift mechanism I prescribed — exhaustive no-`..` destructure in `fill_full_return` — only protects members that exist. A packet whose Schedule D fills lines 3/10 with no 8949 attached, whose Schedule 2 L4 is nonzero with no SE attached, or whose Schedule A L12 > $500 with no 8283 ("You must attach Form 8283 if over $500" — `f1040sa.pdf` line 12 text) is an incomplete return under §7.1's own sentence: "a non-DRAFT return never shows a line with no backing form."

**Ruling: genuine defect, blocks P6.** `PrintedReturn` gains three members (presence rules in Q2/Q5/Q6); the packet's copies of these forms print whole dollars; the crypto-slice exports remain exact-cents and untouched.

One thing you got slightly imprecise, worth correcting for the record: this is **two** defects, not one — (a) the packet omission (SPEC §7.1 coverage), and (b) the rounding-regime mismatch *if* the slice fillers were reused to cure (a). Fixing (a) by reuse would have manufactured (b). The fix below cures both at once.

## Q4 — The 8949 row rule (the detail most likely to be silently wrong)

**Rule: round columns (d) and (e) at the cell with `round_dollar`; column (h) is DERIVED from the printed cells, h = d − e (+ g, which is always blank/zero in v1 — `forms.rs:66-70`). Column totals sum the printed rows. Never round (h) independently from the exact gain.**

Your example was chosen to pass; here is the failing case for independent-h. Row: proceeds d = $100.49, basis e = $0.50, exact gain = $99.99.

- Cell-rounded: d → 100, e → 1 (half-up).
- Independent h: round(99.99) = **100**. But printed d − e = **99**. The row violates the form's own column-(h) instruction — "Subtract column (e) from column (d) and combine the result with column (g)" (TY2024 `f8949.pdf`, column-(h) header) — by $1, and it does so *visibly, on every audited row*.
- Derived h: **99**. The row self-checks.

Why derivation is also what the totals need: line 2 says "Add the amounts in columns (d), (e), (g), and (h)". With derived h, Σh = Σd − Σe *exactly* (integer identity), so the totals row satisfies the same subtract-and-combine relation — and so does **Schedule D line 3**, whose columns carry the same headers ("(h) Gain or (loss): Subtract column (e) from column (d) and combine the result with column (g)" — `schedule_d.pdf` Part I header). Independent-h drifts Σh from Σd − Σe by up to $0.50 × n rows, and Schedule D Part I would fail its own printed arithmetic.

So: **which does the IRS's own arithmetic require?** Derived — both column-(h) texts define h *by subtraction of the printed cells*. **Which cross-foots into Schedule D?** Derived, for the same integer-identity reason. The two questions have the same answer; there is no tension to trade off.

Three subsidiary points:

1. **The derived total gain may differ from `round_dollar(exact_gain)` by several dollars** on many-row years. That is inherent to electing row-level rounding, it is what a human rounding by hand produces, and it flows (via D3) into printed Schedule D L3h/L7/L16 — while the *tax* is still computed from the frozen exact-cents engine and rounded at 1040 L16. That is the known, documented §3.1 regime (printed chain ≠ exact computation by rounding), not a new divergence class. Say so in LIMITATIONS.
2. **Sign formatting on loss rows:** column (h) has no pre-printed parentheses, so a leading minus (the slice's existing rendering) is acceptable under §3.2; run it through the map's `neg` policy for the full path and extend the negative-cell read-back to one 8949 loss row (§3.2 requires a verified negative cell per form). Nit-tier.
3. **NoGainNoLoss dual-basis legs** (basis == proceeds, `forms.rs:93-95`): d = e ⇒ derived h = 0. Consistent by construction; include one in the KAT fixture.

**KAT (row rule):** a two-row fixture containing the d=100.49/e=0.50 row; assert each printed row satisfies h = d − e, and that the part total h equals total d − total e.

## Q3 — Schedule D ← 8949: confirmed, and its Schedule-2 sibling

**Confirmed.** Today `schedule_d_lines` rounds the *exact aggregate*: `line3_d = round_dollar(p.st_proceeds_3d)` (`printed.rs:434`), where `st_proceeds_3d` is the raw cents sum from the projection (`return_1040.rs:970-978`, sourced from `forms::schedule_d`, `forms.rs:172-190`). Once a whole-dollar 8949 is attached, its printed column-(d) total is Σ round_dollar(row) — and Σ(round) ≠ round(Σ) by dollars. Under the composition rule ("chains compose on the PRINTED lines," `printed.rs:11-15`), and under Schedule D line 3's own text ("Totals for all transactions reported on Form(s) 8949 with Box C checked"), **lines 3(d)/(e)/(h) and 10(d)/(e)/(h) must be the 8949's printed column totals.**

**What changes, exactly:**

- `printed::form_8949_printed(rows: &[Form8949Row]) -> Option<Printed8949>` — new, in core. Per-row: d/e rounded, h derived (Q4). Per-part grand totals: Σ of printed columns. `None` when the year has no disposal rows (a carryover/distribution-only Schedule D files with lines 3/10 blank and no 8949 — presence rule).
- `schedule_d_lines(ar: &AbsoluteReturn, f8949: Option<&Printed8949>) -> ScheduleDLines` — signature change. `line3_d/e/h` and `line10_d/e/h` transcribe the printed part totals (zero when `None`); **and the interior netting goes printed-operand**: `line7 = line3_h − line6` and `line15 = line10_h + line13 − line14` (today both re-round exact nets, `printed.rs:438,445` — the same defect one level down; line 7's own text is "Combine lines 1a through 6 in column (h)", an addition chain over printed cells). `line16 = line7 + line15` already composes (`printed.rs:447`). Routing logic unchanged, now driven by the printed line 16; `line21 = min(|line16|, 3000/1500)` computed on printed operands rather than `round_dollar(p.loss_deduction_21)`.
- `assemble_printed_return` builds `f8949` first and threads it (`packet.rs:355`).

**The discriminating KAT:** two short-term rows with proceeds $100.50 and $200.50 (basis 0). Printed rows: 101 + 201 ⇒ printed column total **302**; `round_dollar(301.00) = 301`. Assert `sch_d.line3_d == 302` and `!= round_dollar(exact)` — KAT-9 one level deeper, exactly as you framed it. Add the mirror on (h) via a `.49/.50` basis mix.

**The sibling you did not list but the same ruling forces:** `schedule_2_lines` line 4 is today `round_dollar(sch2_line4_se(ar.se))` = round of the exact `ss + medicare` sum (`printed.rs:59`; `other_taxes.rs:31-33`). Once Schedule SE files as a printed form, Sch 2 L4 must be **SE's printed line 12** (see Q5), or the schedule disagrees with the attachment its line names — the precise analog of the L11↔8959 KAT. This is a change to *shipped* code and needs its own discriminating KAT (ss = X.50, medicare = Y.50 ⇒ printed 10+11 differs from round(exact sum) by $1).

## Q5 — Schedule SE's printed chain, and the SPEC sentence that must change

**The SPEC contradiction is real, and here is the sentence.** SPEC §3.1 (`design/SPEC_full_return.md:121-123`): "unprinted worksheets (QDCGT, IRA/student-loan, §170(b) ceiling, excess-SS, **SE**) carry cents, round once at the value landing on a form line". §7.1 (`:416`) simultaneously lists "**SE**, 8949, 8283 (existing)" in the fill set. A form that is *filed* cannot be an *unprinted worksheet*; its lines are printed lines under the election. **Amendment: strike "SE" from the §3.1 worksheet list** and add: *"the SE engine (`se.rs`) remains an unprinted cents worksheet; Schedule SE itself is a filed form with a printed chain (`printed::schedule_se_lines`) whose lines round the engine's values at the line."* The engine stays FROZEN — nothing here touches `se.rs`; the chain consumes `SeTaxResult` (`se.rs:24-46`) read-only, exactly as `form_8959_lines` already does (`other_taxes.rs:169`).

**Which lines print** (matching the shipped slice filler's line set, `schedule_se.rs:81-94`, and the 2024 form): 2, 3, 4a, 4c, 6, 8a, and — unless 8a ≥ the wage base — 8d, 9, 10; then 11, 12, 13. Line 7 is pre-printed 168,600 on the TY2024 form (verified: `schedule_se.pdf:32-33`; no write, no prefill-exempt needed for 2024). Lines 1a/1b, 4b, 5a/5b, 8b/8c, Part II stay blank.

**Chain rules** (the house rules already in force, applied consistently):

- **Engine values round once at the line:** `line4a = line4c = line6 = round_dollar(se.base)`; `line10 = round_dollar(se.ss)`; `line11 = round_dollar(se.medicare)`; `line13 = round_dollar(se.deductible_half)`; `line8a = round_dollar(owner's box3+box7)`. This is the `form_8959_lines` precedent (line 8 = `round_dollar(se.base)`, `other_taxes.rs:169`) — and it is what makes **SE line 6 ≡ 8959 line 8** hold *by construction*, which the 8959's own line-8 citation requires.
- **Addition/subtraction lines derive from printed operands:** `line12 = line10 + line11` (the form: "Add lines 10 and 11" — KAT-9 class); `line9 = 168,600 − line8d`, floored.
- **Citation lines compose:** `line2 = line3 = sch_c.line31` (the form: "Net profit or (loss) from **Schedule C, line 31**" — `schedule_se.pdf:21-22`; the attached Schedule C's printed 31 and SE's line 2 must be the same integer, so the chain takes `ScheduleCLines` as an argument, not `round_dollar(net_se)` recomputed).
- **§6017:** `schedule_se_lines` returns `Option`, `None` when there is no SE — and core *already* floors: `ar.se` is dropped below $400 base in `return_1040.rs:923` (SPEC §5 stage 7, `:370-371`), so the chain simply mirrors `ar.se`. The packet member is `sch_se: Option<ScheduleSeLines>`; when `None`, Sch 2 L4 = 0 and Schedule 2 may itself be `None`.
- **Identity:** Schedule SE's header is "Name of person **with self-employment income**" + that person's SSN — the **proprietor** (`ReturnHeader.proprietor`, `packet.rs:218-221`), *not* `name_line`. The slice filler writes no identity at all; the full path must, via an `Option<IdentityCells>` on `ScheduleSeMap` failing closed on `None` — the `ScheduleDMap.identity` precedent (`schedule_d_full.rs:266-271`).

**The four tie-outs to pin as KATs** (all on the assembled `PrintedReturn`): `sch_2.line4 == sch_se.line12` (discriminating fixture, see Q3); `sch_1.line15 == sch_se.line13` (holds by construction since `half_se_15` *is* `se.deductible_half`, `return_1040.rs:953,1031` — pin it anyway, it is one refactor from breaking); `f8959.line8 == sch_se.line6`; `sch_se.line2 == sch_c.line31`.

**One decision to record, with its alternative:** line 13's form text is "Multiply line 12 by 50%". I rule `line13 = round_dollar(se.deductible_half)` (engine value at the line; keeps §164(f) faithful to the statute and makes the Sch 1 tie-out automatic). The alternative — `round_dollar(line12 / 2)` on printed operands — can differ by $1 (e.g. ss = 10.50, medicare = 11.50 ⇒ printed 12 = 23, half = 12 vs `round_dollar(11.00) = 11`) and would force `schedule_1_lines` to take the SE chain as an argument. A human recomputing "50% of line 12" may land $1 off my chosen rendering; that residual is the same accepted class as 8959 line 7's shipped behavior, and I accept it knowingly. Document in the chain's doc comment.

## Q2 — The shape: core chains + parallel full fillers; share only below the arithmetic waterline

**Ruling: option (a) — printed chains in core, separate full-path fillers, slice fillers untouched. No rounding-mode parameter.** A mode parameter is the unified filler wearing a disguise: it puts both regimes behind one entry point, which is precisely the "future harmonization" surface prior-Q4 ruled out. The slice's cents rendering is load-bearing shipped behavior (CSV-identity: `fmt_money`, `lib.rs:66-71`; `row_cells` at `fill8949.rs:35-50`; byte-goldens) serving 2017/2025 and no-`ReturnInputs` years.

Per form:

- **Form 8949.** Core: `Printed8949` (rows + per-part grand totals — Q3/Q4). Forms: a new full entry that builds `PartData` from the printed chain and **reuses `fill8949::fill_8949_parts`** (`fill8949.rs:140-187`). This is not filler unification: `PartData` is pre-formatted *strings* (`fill8949.rs:17-24`) — rounding-agnostic — and `fill_8949_parts` is pure geometry/verification. What the full path must *not* reuse is `part_data` (`fill8949.rs:53-74`), which sums exact decimals in the forms crate — slice-only. **Pagination:** the chain stays page-agnostic; the full filler chunks rows at `map.rows_per_page` (14 on TY2024, `f8949.map.toml:3`) and writes per-page line-2 totals by summing that page's *whole-dollar* rows. Sanctioned despite "zero arithmetic in forms": partitioned integer sums cannot re-diverge (no rounding remains — Σ page-totals ≡ core's grand total, an associativity identity), and page capacity is legitimately the map's datum, not core's. Pin it anyway: a read-back KAT asserting the pages' printed totals sum to the chain's grand totals, and the Schedule D L3d *cell text* equals the 8949 grand-total figure (the cross-PDF oracle from my prior Q2). Identity: `Option<IdentityCells>` on `Form8949Map` (name_line + taxpayer SSN — "Name(s) shown on return"), fail-closed on the full path; the slice never writes it, unchanged.
- **Schedule SE.** A parallel `schedule_se_full.rs` transcribing `ScheduleSeLines` + proprietor identity — the exact `schedule_d.rs`/`schedule_d_full.rs` precedent, already reviewed and shipped for this precise situation. Do not refactor the slice filler's plan-building (`schedule_se.rs:77-107` computes line 9 and line 12 *in the filler*, in cents — grandfathered slice behavior protected by byte-goldens; the full path must not inherit it, and a shared-tail refactor risks the goldens for no gain at ~60 lines of geometry).
- **Form 8283.** Core produces a newtype `Printed8283Rows(Vec<Form8283Row>)` with the three money fields (`cost_basis`, `fmv`, `claimed_deduction`) `round_dollar`ed — the wrapper (not a bare `Vec` of the same type) so a cents row *cannot* be handed to the full filler by accident. Forms: a full entry reusing `fill_one`/`group_section_b` (`form8283.rs:304-440`, arithmetic-free — grouping and transcription), plus the **filer identity block** the slice never writes ("Name(s) shown on your income tax return" + identifying number → `name_line` + taxpayer SSN; `Option<IdentityCells>` on `Form8283Map`, fail-closed on the full path). `round_dollar` yields scale-0 decimals, so the shared `push_money`/`fmt_money` path renders "12345" with no formatter change (`conventions.rs:37-39`).

`PrintedReturn` additions: `f8949: Option<Printed8949>`, `sch_se: Option<ScheduleSeLines>`, `f8283: Option<Printed8283Rows>`. The no-`..` destructure in `fill_full_return` then forces the three fillers at compile time — the mechanism working as designed, *once the members exist*.

## Q6 — Form 8283: same election, different tie-out

**Same rounding treatment (D7), but do NOT force Sch A L12 == Σ 8283.** The asymmetry with Schedule D is principled, and the rule generalizes: **a line whose form text cites another form as its *source* composes on that form's printed line (Sch D L3/L10 ← 8949; Sch 2 L4 ← SE L12; Sch 1 L15 ← SE L13; 8959 L8 ← SE L6); a line that merely *requires an attachment* does not re-derive from it.** Schedule A L12's text is "Other than by cash or check… You must attach Form 8283 if over $500" — an attachment requirement, not a source citation. Form 8283 has **no grand-total line** for L12 to equal, and the §170(b) ceilings legitimately make L12 *less* than the sum of the 8283's per-donation amounts (the excess becomes carryover — `charitable.rs` runs in cents as an unprinted worksheet, correctly). Keep `line12 = round_dollar(p.charitable_noncash_12)` (`printed.rs:716`). Document in LIMITATIONS: a hand-sum of the 8283's whole-dollar cells may differ from L12 by sub-dollar rounding besides any ceiling effect; neither is a defect the form defines.

**Presence rule:** `f8283 = Some` iff the return itemizes (`sch_a.is_some()`) **and** printed `sch_a.line12 > 500` **and** the ledger has donation rows. KAT both directions (a standard-deduction year with donations files no 8283).

**Two flagged uncertainties, verify against i8283/Pub 526 at implementation — do not take my word:** (i) whether a *carryover* year (noncash claimed only via L13) requires re-attaching an 8283 — I believe not for v1's shape, but pin it; (ii) **a genuine coverage gap the sweep exposed**: `ScheduleAInputs.charitable` accepts *non-crypto noncash* gifts (`CharitableClass::CapGainProp*/OrdinaryProp*`, `return_inputs.rs:220-233,286`) which reach L12 but for which btctax can produce **no 8283 rows** (no property details exist). If such gifts push noncash over $500, the packet would attach an 8283 that under-reports its own Section A/B property list — an incomplete required attachment, §170(f)(11) denial risk. **Ruling: refuse at export** (new §4.10-style guard: non-crypto noncash gift present ∧ L12 > $500 ⇒ refuse with "complete Form 8283 for your non-crypto property by hand" messaging), as a spec amendment alongside the others. Fail-closed beats a silently under-populated substantiation form; the carve-out (§3.4) does not apply because the omission is not taxpayer-favorable.
