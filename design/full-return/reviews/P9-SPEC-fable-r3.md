## Critical

### C-1 — The re-scoped HSA prompt covers two of the four Form 8889 triggers; a truthful "No" from a testing-period filer computes an understated return, and "scoped to the Form 8889 triggers" is an unmet stated guarantee

**What.** §2.4's prompt — *"did you (or your employer) contribute to, or did you take a distribution from, a health savings account?"* — with `Some(false)` ⇒ **proceed**. The Form 8889 filing triggers (instructions "Who Must File" — persuasive; each backed by statute — law) are four, not two:

1. Contributions by **anyone** — "you (or someone on your behalf, including your employer)". §223(a) makes a third-party contribution the account holder's own for deduction purposes. The prompt's "you (or your employer)" lets a filer whose parent funded their HSA answer "No" truthfully.
2. Distributions received — covered ✓.
3. **Testing-period income inclusion** — a filer who used the §223(b)(8) last-month rule (or a §408(d)(9) IRA-to-HSA funding distribution) in year Y−1 and ceased to be an eligible individual in year Y must, **in year Y**, include the shortfall in gross income **plus a 10% additional tax** — §223(b)(8)(B)(i)–(ii), §408(d)(9)(D) (statute, i.e. law). In year Y this filer may have made **no contribution and taken no distribution**. They answer the prompt "No" — truthfully — and btctax computes a return that omits both the income and the 10% additional tax.
4. **Acquisition by death of the account beneficiary** — a non-spouse beneficiary must include the account's FMV in gross income (§223(f)(8)(B)) and files 8889; they took no "distribution" in lay terms and plausibly answer "No".

**Why it blocks.** Trigger 3 is a silent understatement for a population squarely inside the archetype (an employee who had HDHP coverage, used the last-month rule, and changed jobs in January). §2.4's own indictment — *"an unasked filer … gets a return that omits the income AND the penalty"* — applies verbatim to a **mis-asked** filer. And §2.4 states flatly *"the question is scoped to the Form 8889 triggers"* — false as written. Under the ladder (wrong tax result in the understating direction + an unmet stated guarantee), and consistent with r2 grading the mirror-image mis-scope of this same field Critical, this is Critical. (Floor if the author disputes population size: Important — but the guarantee sentence still has to change.)

**Evidence.** §223(b)(8)(B); §408(d)(9)(D); §223(f)(8)(B); §223(a). Form 8889 instructions, "Who Must File" (persuasive). The spec's own §2.4 text and §4 bullet 3.

**Fix (spec-level, small).** Widen the prompt to the trigger set, e.g.: *"In {year}, did anyone (including your employer) put money into a health savings account for you; did you take or inherit money from one; or do you owe testing-period income because you stopped being HSA-eligible after a prior-year last-month-rule contribution or IRA-to-HSA funding distribution?"* — `Some(true)` ⇒ refuse unsupported (unchanged), `Some(false)` ⇒ proceed (a dormant holder still answers "No" to all clauses). If the author judges triggers 3–4 too exotic for one prompt, the spec must instead disclaim them in writing with a LIMITATIONS entry and an owning phase — the r1-I5 standard the spec itself adopted. Either way §2.4's "scoped to the Form 8889 triggers" sentence and the §4 bullet must match what is actually asked.

### I-1 — §2.6's `unlaunder(sch1.hsa_activity)` is written against a key that does not exist in a v1 blob: the rename has no specified mechanism, and the naive implementation makes the acceptance's own mutation-check unsatisfiable

**What.** A v1 blob carries `"hsa_present": false|true` (bare `bool`, always serialized — `return_inputs.rs:319-321`). §2.4 renames the field to `hsa_activity` **and** changes its type. §2.6 then unlaunders `sch1.hsa_activity` — but `row_to_inputs` (`crates/btctax-cli/src/return_inputs.rs:50-66`) deserializes **first**, then fixes fields in memory. With no `deny_unknown_fields` anywhere, deserializing a v1 blob into the new struct **drops `hsa_present` on the floor** and defaults `hsa_activity` to `None`:

- A stored `false` becomes `None` — the right end state, **by accident**, with the specced `unlaunder` a no-op.
- A stored **typed `true`** (real: `income import` stores unscreened — P8a M3 — so `hsa_present = true` blobs exist in the model) is **silently discarded**: the filer's typed answer is lost and the year flips from refuses-as-unsupported to refuses-as-unanswered. Data loss of an answer — the D-8 family.
- §4's *"drop any `unlaunder` in the v1→v2 migration → a named test fails"* is **unsatisfiable for this key**: with no rename mapping, the hsa unlaunder is dead code, and deleting dead code fails nothing. The named v1-blob test would pass vacuously — the exact P8a-I2 shape, rebuilt inside the migration that cites P8a I2.
- The cited fixture pattern cannot even *build* the fixture: P8a's `v0_blob` serializes the **current** struct and rewrites keys (`return_inputs.rs:247-262`), but the current struct will no longer emit `hsa_present`, so there is no `"hsa_present":null` to rewrite and the `assert_ne!` guard fires.

The other three keys are clean (`blind` ×2 and `salt_use_sales_tax` keep their names; stored `false` → `Some(false)` → unlaundered), and I verified **no other field changes type**: the flip list {hsa, taxpayer-blind, spouse-blind, salt} is exactly right, `dual_status_alien` and the mortgage flag are new fields (honest `None`), and the three deleted fields need no unlaundering.

**Why it blocks.** The migration is the section the spec calls load-bearing ("or the laundering ships"), its stated mutation-check guarantee cannot be met as written, and each of the three viable mechanisms has consequences the spec must pin — this is precisely where this program has now been burned three times (D-8, P8a I2, r2 I-1).

**Evidence.** `crates/btctax-cli/src/return_inputs.rs:50-66` (deserialize-then-fixup), `:247-262` (fixture pattern); `return_inputs.rs:319-321`; `cmd/tax.rs:104`; serde unknown-key default behavior.

**Fix.** Spec the mechanism explicitly. Cleanest: **migrate at the raw-JSON level** — in `row_to_inputs`, for `version < 2`, read `sch1.hsa_present` out of the raw `serde_json::Value` before typed deserialization and write `hsa_activity: true→Some(true), false→None` (then the in-memory unlaunder list shrinks to the three same-name keys). This keeps the TOML surface clean (`hsa_present` in a TOML is then an unknown key and refuses under §2.3 — correct). Alternative: `#[serde(alias = "hsa_present")]` + unlaunder — but then the spec must also state whether the §2.3 known-key walk admits the alias (it leaks into the TOML surface). Either way: the v1 fixture for this key must be a **literal v1 JSON** (or built from a shape that still owns the old key), and the named test must cover both `false → None` **and** `true → Some(true)`.

**Evidence.** §2.1 vs §2.2 of the artifact; `packet.rs:203-215` (blind boxes printed from `Person.blind`); `form1040_full.rs:377` (CTC/ODC deliberately unchecked, L19 = $0).

### I-5 — The classifier's acceptance bullet claims a force the stated `_` rule cannot deliver: `field: _name` (or any named binding) defeats it in one token — verified by compile experiment

**What.** §4: *"A new `bool`… anywhere reachable… **does not compile until registered or exempted with its class and statutory reason**."* The §3.3 rule forbids `_` on structs/collections and classifiable leaves. But `w2s: _w2s` is not `_`, compiles silently, and recurses into nothing; `blind: _b` "classifies" nothing. Compile experiment (scratchpad, rustc 2021): `let Inputs { w2s: _w2s, flag } = ri;` — clean; `let Inputs { w2s: whatever, … }` with `whatever` unused — **hard error under `#[deny(unused_variables)]`**, which the spec does not mention. The true compiler-held force — which the spec's own ⚠️ "honest limit" paragraph correctly states — is only: *a new field is a compile error until a human touches the pattern*. Registration/exemption-with-reason is review convention. §4's bullet and §3.3's honest-limit paragraph are in direct contradiction, and §4 is the gate.

**Why it blocks.** An acceptance criterion that cannot be true invites either a false green-claim or a re-litigation round — and this is the exact "the guarantee is narrower than the rhetoric" finding (r1 I-3) in its third round.

**Evidence.** Compile experiment above; `first_negative_amount`'s own `header: _` (`return_refuse.rs:204`) as the standing precedent that patterns hold decisions, not correctness.

**Fix.** (1) Strengthen the mechanism: the classifier module carries `#![deny(unused_variables)]` (or the workspace lint table), and the rule becomes *"`_` **and `_`-prefixed bindings** are forbidden on structs, collections, and classifiable leaves — an ignoring named binding is then a hard error, and the only remaining evasion (`let _ = x;`) is grep-able review residue."* (2) Reword the §4 bullet to the true guarantee: *"a new field anywhere reachable does not compile until a human edits the classifier; the edit is reviewed against the exemption register, which carries class + statutory reason."*
